Niger-Area

  Nigeria was given her name by the wife of the first british colonial master;Lord Fedrick Lugard. Nigeria was formerly called Niger-area.
  Nigeria as a country was colonized by the british and after her colonisation she has been independent till date;(since 1960-till date).
  The day Nigeria got free from the administration of the british was 1st,october 1960, and that day was tagged till now as Nigeria's Independence day.

National Symbols.

  1. The national flag

    • The Nigeria flag has two colors which are green and white
    • It was designed by a young scholar named Taiwo Akinkunmi Michael in 1959.
    • The color Green signifies natural wealth;while the White signifies peace.



  2. The coat of arm


    The Nigerian Coat of Arm consists of the following:

    A Black Shield with a white Pall
    The white pall symbolizies the meeting of the Niger and Benue rivers at lokoja
    while the black shield represents Nigeria's fertile soil.
    Two supporting Horses on each sides
    This represents dignity.
    The Eagle
    The Eagle represents strength
    The Green and White Twists at the top of the torse of the shield
    This represents the rich soil.
    The Red Flowers
    This is found at the base and is often reffered to as "costus spectabilis". This signifies the beauty of the nation.
    The national motto
    This is found on the bandrole around the base. motto:Unity and Faith,Peace and Progress.
